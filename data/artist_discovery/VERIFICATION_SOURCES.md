## Tier 1: Institutional Oral History Archives

### Smithsonian Archives of American Art
- **2,300+ artist interviews** since 1958
- First interviews: Abraham Walkowitz, Charles Sheeler, Edward Hopper
- Transcripts available online since 2001
- **URL**: https://www.aaa.si.edu/collections/about-the-oral-history-program
- **Excerpts**: https://www.aaa.si.edu/resources/excerpts-from-the-archives-oral-history-interviews

### MoMA Archives - Artist Oral History Initiative
- **100+ oral histories** filmed in galleries
- Artists discuss works with curators/scholars
- Grant-funded initiative since 2011
- **URL**: https://www.moma.org/research/archives/oral-history

### RISD Oral History Project
- Faculty, alumni, visiting artists
- Equity, inclusion, sustainability topics
- Video interviews with transcripts
- **URL**: https://digitalcommons.risd.edu/archives_oralhistories/
- **Museum Records**: https://risdmuseum.org/art-design/projects-publications/research/collection-research

### Artists Archives of the Western Reserve
- Videotaped artist interviews
- Transcribed for researchers
- **URL**: https://www.artistsarchives.org/our-mission/oral-history-program/

## Search Strategy for Each Artist

### For Quotes (10 required per artist)
1. Search Smithsonian AAA for oral history transcript
2. Search archive.org for `"artist name" interview`
3. Check museum archives for lecture recordings
4. Verify against primary source before inclusion

### For Biography
1. Cross-reference multiple sources
2. Verify dates against institutional records
3. Document major works with provenance

### For Visual Reference
1. Museum collection images (rights-cleared)
2. Archive.org historical photos
3. Wikimedia Commons (verify license)
