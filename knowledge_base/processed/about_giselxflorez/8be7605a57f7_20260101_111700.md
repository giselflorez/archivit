## Content
**Gisel Florez** is a **digital artist** specializing in manual camera and video techniques combined with conceptual practice.[1] She explores interconnectedness and existence through the visual study of waves, focusing on the relationship between light and time.[1]

With over 17 years of experience in photography in New York City, Florez has worked as a commercial still-life photographer for major brands including L'Oréal, Revlon, ESPN, and The New York Times, as well as publications like V Magazine and Surface.[2] She transitioned into the digital art space around 2018, inspired by blockchain technology and developments in crypto art.[2]

Her current work primarily involves **NFTs and video clips**.[2] She is also known for creating large-scale prints and has recently explored analog color photography and darkroom techniques.[2]

Beyond her individual practice, Florez is a **co-founder of WOCA (Women of Crypto Art)** and a RISD Photo Affinity Leader.[1][2] Her work has been exhibited at numerous venues including NFTNYC, Winkleman Gallery, the Museum of Crypto Art, and virtual exhibitions in Cryptovoxels.[1][2] She is featured as one of 50 artists in "CRYPTO ART - Begins," the first NFT book documenting the evolution of crypto art.[4]
