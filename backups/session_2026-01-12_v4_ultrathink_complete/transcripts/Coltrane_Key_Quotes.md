## VERIFIED KEY QUOTES

### On Purpose (Frank Kofsky Interview, 1966)
> **COLTRANE:** "I want to be a force for real good. In other words, I know that there are bad forces, forces that bring suffering to others and misery to the world, but I want to be the opposite force. I want to be the force which is truly for good."

*Source: Frank Kofsky Interview, November 1966 (Audio recording, Pacifica Radio Archives)*

### On Spiritual Expression
> **COLTRANE:** "My music is the spiritual expression of what I am - my faith, my knowledge, my being."

*Source: Liner notes, A Love Supreme (1965)*

### On Practice
> **COLTRANE:** "You can play a shoestring if you're sincere."

*Source: Various interviews*

### On God (Liner Notes)
> **COLTRANE:** "I think the main thing a musician would like to do is to give a picture to the listener of the many wonderful things he knows of and senses in the universe."

*Source: A Love Supreme liner notes, handwritten by Coltrane (1964)*

## A LOVE SUPREME POEM (1964)
*Handwritten by Coltrane as liner notes*

> "During the year 1957, I experienced, by the grace of God, a spiritual awakening which was to lead me to a richer, fuller, more productive life. At that time, in gratitude, I humbly asked to be given the means and privilege to make others happy through music..."

## JAZZ CASUAL APPEARANCE (Dec 7, 1963)

**Host:** Ralph J. Gleason
**Tracks Performed:** "Afro Blue," "Alabama," "Impressions"
**Note:** Coltrane refused on-camera interview but agreed to perform

**Key Observation:** His dedication to the music speaks louder than words. The "Alabama" performance (written after the 16th Street Baptist Church bombing) demonstrates music as spiritual and political expression.
