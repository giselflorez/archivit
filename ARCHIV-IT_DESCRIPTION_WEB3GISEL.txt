Your Data. Your Future. Take Control.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
THE PROBLEM UNDERSTOOD
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

For over a decade, digital artists have created work across an expanding
constellation of platforms, blockchains, and formats. We have minted on Ethereum
when it was revolutionary, expanded to Tezos for accessible editions, explored
Solana for experimentation, and even returned to Bitcoin's Counterparty protocol
for historical significance. We have written artist statements on platforms that
no longer exist, given interviews that disappeared behind paywalls, and watched
our exhibition documentation scatter across the digital wind.

The result: fragmentation.

Your creative journey exists in pieces. Your words about your own work are
separated from the work itself. Your mint history across multiple wallets tells
a story no one can easily read. The context that makes art meaningful - the
why behind the what - becomes increasingly difficult to preserve.

ARCHIV-IT was built to solve this.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
WHO BUILT THIS AND WHY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

ARCHIV-IT was created by The Founder (GISELX), a New York-based artist with
seventeen years in photographic arts. A graduate of the Rhode Island School of
Design, Gisel has built a practice centered on the study of light and time
through manual camera techniques.

In 2018, inspired by developments in blockchain technology, Gisel became one of
the first wave of crypto artists - tokenizing work on SuperRare and KnownOrigin,
exploring immutable technology across Ethereum, Tezos, Solana, and beyond. As
co-founder of WOCA (Women of Crypto Art) and art advisor to The Verse Verse,
she has dedicated herself to expanding artistic community and bridging creative
practice with emerging technology.

This software emerges from that lived experience. Not from speculation. Not from
trading. From the daily reality of maintaining a creative practice across an
increasingly complex digital landscape.

As Gisel has stated throughout development:

    "Everything has to be classy and not wordy."

    "I want them to have fun but in a very single stream, quick and
     efficient way."

    "More important is the artist creations, interviews, articles, and
     exhibition information that it will be aggregating."

    "Your Data. Your Output. Take Control."


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
WHAT ARCHIV-IT DOES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

ARCHIV-IT is a local-first archive system that runs entirely on your machine.
Nothing leaves unless you want it to. No cloud servers storing your research.
No companies monetizing your curatorial intelligence.

KNOWLEDGE BASE
Import and preserve everything that contextualizes your work: artist statements,
interviews, exhibition documentation, research notes, creative process records.
Search semantically across everything you have collected. Your words about your
work, permanently connected to the work itself.

MULTI-BLOCKCHAIN TRACKING
Eight blockchain networks supported with intelligent auto-detection. Paste any
wallet address - Ethereum, Tezos, Solana, Bitcoin, Counterparty, Fake Rares,
EOS, WAX - and the system recognizes the format automatically. Track up to
three wallet addresses per artist profile. See your complete mint history
unified in one view.

VISUAL TRANSLATOR
Navigate your archive visually. Thumbnails at a glance, like a digital
lightroom. Filter by platform, network, content type. Hover for metadata.
Click through to original sources. Designed for artists managing thousands
of assets, not dozens.

SEMANTIC NETWORK
Visualize the connections between your documents, tags, and themes. A point
cloud of vertices connected by data relationships - clickable, draggable,
responsive. Floating icons in a net of meaning that expands as your archive
grows.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
DESIGN PHILOSOPHY: WHY IT FEELS DIFFERENT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Most NFT tools feel like trading terminals. Charts and numbers optimized for
speculation. Green and red signaling profit and loss. The artwork itself
becomes secondary to its market performance.

ARCHIV-IT rejects this aesthetic entirely.

The interface uses a near-black background (#0a0a0a) that recalls gallery walls.
Gold accents (#d4a574) connote value and craftsmanship rather than market heat.
Typography references both the art world (Crimson Pro for headers, evoking
museum placards) and modern precision (Geist for body text, clean and minimal).

Interactive elements respond with subtle animations - shining gradients that
draw the eye without overwhelming. Growing underlines that respond to your
input. Privacy notices that reveal on hover rather than cluttering the screen.

The goal, consistently expressed throughout development:

    "Clean, minimal but fully intuitive interface with expert features
     to sort and parse the information."

    "Think like Lightroom with thumbnails of a large project."

    "A responsive nature of icons floating in a net of data."

    "Feels like an art installation, not software."

This is not accidental. Every design decision serves the same principle:
respect the artwork, serve the artist.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
WHY THIS MATTERS: THE FRAGMENTATION PROBLEM
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Consider the challenge facing any serious digital artist today:

You have minted 200 works across three blockchains over five years. Your
statements about those works exist in Twitter threads (some deleted), Medium
posts (one platform already deprecated), email interviews (scattered across
inboxes), exhibition catalogs (some only available in print), and marketplace
descriptions (edited multiple times, original versions lost).

A collector researching your work has no way to understand your creative
evolution. A curator preparing an exhibition cannot access the context that
makes your practice meaningful. A journalist writing about your contribution
to the field has to piece together fragments from a dozen sources.

You yourself, looking back on your own journey, struggle to remember what you
were thinking when you made that pivotal series three years ago.

This is not a hypothetical. This is the lived reality of digital artists right
now.

ARCHIV-IT addresses this by creating a single, searchable repository where:

- Your mint timeline across all chains lives in one unified view
- Your statements, interviews, and documentation connect to specific works
- Your tags and themes reveal patterns in your practice over time
- Your research on other artists and movements builds your contextual knowledge
- Everything stays on your machine, under your control, forever

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
FUTURE VISION: WHERE THIS IS GOING
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The foundation exists today. What comes next builds on this architecture:

SEMANTIC SEARCH
Natural language queries across your entire archive. "Find where I discussed
organic forms and natural systems" returns relevant documents, ranked by
relevance, without requiring exact keyword matches.

VISUAL SIMILARITY
CLIP embeddings for all images enable queries like "find artworks visually
similar to this piece" - discovering connections across different series,
different years, different platforms.

LOCAL AI INTEGRATION
Local language models (Llama 3) generate promotional materials from your
archive while maintaining your authentic voice. Exhibition texts, press
releases, grant applications - all synthesized from your own documented words
rather than generic templates.

CREATIVE PERIOD DETECTION
Algorithms that analyze your mint timeline and visual evolution to automatically
identify distinct creative periods. "Geometric Experiments (2021)" flows into
"Organic Transition (2022)" - your artistic journey mapped without manual
curation.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
PRIVACY ARCHITECTURE: WHY LOCAL-FIRST MATTERS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Every tool in the current ecosystem asks you to trust someone else with your
data. Upload your wallet addresses to their servers. Store your notes in their
cloud. Let them see which artists you research, which works you collect, which
patterns you follow.

This data has value. Your curatorial intelligence - knowing which artists
matter before the market recognizes them - is competitive advantage. Most
platforms quietly monetize this.

ARCHIV-IT takes a different approach:

- All data stored on your machine
- No central server
- No analytics or tracking
- API keys stored locally, never transmitted
- Optional cloud features are opt-in, not default

What stays local: your documents, your wallet addresses, your search history,
your connections and tags, your research intelligence.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
THE EMOTIONAL CORE: START THE LEGACY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The setup wizard asks you to "Explore Your Legacy."

This is not marketing language. This is the genuine intent behind the software.

Digital artists working today are creating the art history of the future. The
works minted on blockchain will persist longer than most museum collections.
The challenge is not preservation of the artwork itself - that is solved by
immutable technology - but preservation of context.

Fifty years from now, someone researching early NFT art will have access to
the tokens. Will they have access to why those works were made? What the
artist was thinking? How each piece connected to a larger creative vision?

ARCHIV-IT is infrastructure for that future understanding. Not speculation
tools. Not trading terminals. Documentation systems for artists who understand
that context is what transforms objects into art.

ABOUT THE CREATOR
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

GISEL FLOREZ (GISELX) is a New York-based artist working with manual camera
and video techniques, exploring interconnected natures within the space of
existence through the visual study of waves. With seventeen years in
photographic arts and degrees from the Rhode Island School of Design, her
practice centers on beauty found within light and time.

As one of the first wave of crypto artists, her work appears on SuperRare,
KnownOrigin, Foundation, and other platforms across Ethereum, Tezos, and
multiple chains. She is co-founder of WOCA (Women of Crypto Art), art advisor
to The Verse Verse, and RISD affinity leader.

Her work has been shown at NFTNYC, Winkleman Gallery, Museum of Crypto Art,
and featured in Fortune's TOP50 NFT, WOCA in Vogue, and Coindesk Portrait.


ARCHIV-IT emerges from this practice - built by an artist who understands
the challenges firsthand, designed for the community she has spent years
helping to build.
