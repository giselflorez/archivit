# TORI AMOS (1963-)
## NORTHSTAR Master Profile
### Breath as Structure, Piano as Portal

---

## GLOBAL UNDERSTANDING

**Full Name:** Myra Ellen Amos (Tori Amos)
**Born:** August 22, 1963, Newton, North Carolina, USA
**Profession:** Singer-songwriter, pianist, composer, record producer

**NORTHSTAR:** Music exists as light structures - the artist channels, not creates
**Core Belief:** "The piano plays me, really. I don't play it. There is a surrender to what the instrument is capable of."

---

## CHARACTER & MINDSET

### How She Thinks
- **Synesthesia:** Experiences music as structures of light - "The song appears as light filament once I've cracked it"
- **Channeling:** Songs come through her, not from her - she is a vessel
- **Emotional Archaeology:** Excavates buried feelings through composition
- **Structure-Building:** Distinguishes between players and songwriters - "there are amazing players but they're not structure-builders"
- **Integration of Classical & Confessional:** Merges technical mastery with raw emotional honesty

### Key Concepts
1. **Breath as Rhythm:** Uses breath patterns as rhythmic foundation
2. **The Piano as Partner:** The instrument has its own voice and agency
3. **Light Creatures:** Each song manifests as a unique visual light form
4. **Sonic War:** Bringing the woman-and-piano format into rock spaces
5. **Emotional Excavation:** Mining personal trauma for universal truth

### Her Process
1. LISTEN - Receive the song as it approaches
2. SURRENDER - Let the piano lead, not the ego
3. EXCAVATE - Dig into emotional truth without flinching
4. STRUCTURE - Build the architecture that holds the emotion
5. EMBODY - Use breath, voice, and body as instruments
6. RELEASE - Let the song become its own entity

### Key Songs & Their Techniques

**"Crucify" (1992)**
- Drum part: largely kick drum + low tom "relentless hammering"
- Explores religious guilt and self-punishment

**"Precious Things" (1992)**
- Verses feature eighth-note bursts of breath as rhythm
- Line "so I ran faster" inspires the breathwork

**"Cornflake Girl" (1994)**
- Emerged from improvisation during another recording
- "I was just playing along, and then, when the music stopped, I found myself still playing that riff"

**"Professional Widow" (1996)**
- Remix reached #1 worldwide
- Original was harpsichord-driven, remix became house anthem

## FINE-GRANULAR UNDERSTANDING

### The Bosendorfer Relationship

**The Instrument:**
- Bosendorfer Model 190 Concert Grand Piano
- Austrian-made, prized for rich sound and craftsmanship
- Extended bass range for deeper, more resonant tone
- First Bosendorfer acquired in 1993

**The Philosophy:**
> "All I knew was - when I touched her - that she became my friend. And it's not as if it were one piano, it's any Bosendorfer seems to have some kind of understanding. They can speak, and they can listen. And I think, when you play them, you become an extension."

**Technical Details:**
- Tours with her Bosendorfer
- Uses the piano's extended bass for emotional depth
- Rolling ball bearings on strings for textural effects (Under the Pink sessions)

### Synesthesia and Light Creatures

Tori experiences chromesthesia - seeing music as light:
> "The song appears as light filament once I've cracked it. As long as I've been doing this, which is more than thirty-five years, I've never seen the same light creature in my life."

This informs:
- How she knows when a song is "complete"
- Her arrangement decisions
- Her sense of each song's unique identity

### Child Prodigy Background

- Age 2: Could reproduce pieces heard only once
- Age 3: Composing original songs
- Age 5: Youngest person admitted to Peabody Conservatory
- Expelled at 11 for playing by ear instead of reading music
- "I preferred improvising and playing by ear instead"

### Unconventional Song Structures

Tori's songs often defy standard verse-chorus-verse:
- Sudden tempo shifts mid-song
- Irregular phrase lengths
- "loose 3/4 tempo punctuated with irregular phrases"
- Changes instrumentation to signal emotional shifts

### The "Sonic War"

On bringing piano to rock venues:
> "It took some nerve to bring the woman-and-piano-thing onstage. It was a sonic war."

She was among the first women to tour with a grand piano in pop/rock contexts, fighting for the instrument's place in non-classical settings.
