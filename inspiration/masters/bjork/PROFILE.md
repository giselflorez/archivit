# BJORK
## NORTHSTAR MASTER PROFILE

**Full Name:** Bjork Gudmundsdottir
**Born:** November 21, 1965, Reykjavik, Iceland
**Location:** Reykjavik, Iceland
**Disciplines:** Music Composition, Singing, Production, Multimedia Art, Technology Innovation, Fashion, Film

---

## GLOBAL UNDERSTANDING

### Biography

Bjork is an Icelandic singer, songwriter, record producer, actress, and multimedia artist. She is among the most innovative and influential artists of the past four decades, known for eclectic musical styles spanning electronic, classical, experimental, trip-hop, and avant-garde, as well as pioneering work integrating music with visual art, technology, fashion, and nature.

After gaining attention as lead singer of the alternative rock band The Sugarcubes, Bjork launched her solo career in 1993 with "Debut." She has since released multiple critically acclaimed albums, each representing a distinct artistic world with unique collaborators, visual identities, and conceptual frameworks.

Her work has been honored with the MoMA retrospective (2015), where "Biophilia" became the first app in the museum's permanent collection. She has collaborated with visionaries including Alexander McQueen, Michel Gondry, Spike Jonze, Chris Cunningham, and Matthew Barney.

### NORTHSTAR

**THE UNITY OF NATURE AND TECHNOLOGY** - Bjork's NORTHSTAR is the synthesis of organic and digital, primal and futuristic. She believes that technology and nature are not opposites but expressions of the same creative force. Her mission is creating art that bridges these realms, using cutting-edge technology to express ancient natural truths.

### Core Belief

> "For me, nature and technology stand for hope, and for a movement onwards to the future. If you can make nature and technology friends, then you can make everyone friends; you can make everyone intact."

Bjork sees the apparent opposition between nature and technology as a false dichotomy - both are expressions of creativity and evolution.

## CHARACTER & MINDSET

### How She Thought

Bjork operates from **bio-association consciousness** - the deliberate fusion of seemingly incompatible elements to create new forms. Her mindset combines:

- **Nature as Chapel**: Iceland's volcanic landscapes as primary spiritual reference
- **Technology as Instrument**: Machines as extensions of natural expression, not replacements
- **Emotional Research**: Each album as investigation of specific emotional terrain
- **Collaborative Expansion**: Working with experts in other fields to expand creative vocabulary

**Key Mental Frameworks:**
- **Nature + Technology = Future**: The synthesis creates hope, not alienation
- **Creativity as Creature**: Something alive that moves around - you track it, you don't control it
- **Multimedia Unity**: Sound, vision, touch, interaction - all one art form
- **Emotional Archaeology**: Digging into specific feelings to their deepest level

### Key Concepts

1. **Biophilia**: Album-as-app exploring connections between music, nature, and technology. The word means "love of living systems" - each song explores a natural phenomenon through custom instruments and interactive apps.

2. **Custom Instrumentation**: Creating new instruments for each project - the gameleste, gravity harp, tesla coil synthesizer.

3. **Nature Chapel**: "Nature is our chapel" - Iceland's landscapes as spiritual grounding and creative source.

4. **Multimedia Integration**: Albums are not just music but complete universes of sound, image, costume, performance, and interaction.

### Her Process

**On Creativity:**
> "I think creativity always lives somewhere in everyone but its nature is quite pranksterish and slippery and every time u grab its tail its found a nu corner to thrive in. Perhaps the trick is not to force it and put it up against a wall and want it to be in a particular area, but rather w a lot of kindness sniff it out and wonder where it has gone to this time around."

**Collaboration as Method:**
- Identifies the world's leading practitioners in relevant fields
- Brings them into the creative process as true collaborators, not hired hands
- Allows collision between disciplines to generate new forms
- Film directors for videos, fashion designers for costumes, software engineers for apps

**Emotional Specificity:**
Each album investigates a particular emotional terrain:
- "Vespertine" - intimacy, domestic sounds, interiority
- "Vulnicura" - heartbreak, grief, recovery
- "Utopia" - healing, birdsong, hope

## FINE-GRANULAR UNDERSTANDING

### Specific Techniques

**Custom Instrument Creation:**

**The Gameleste:**
- Hybrid instrument combining celesta and gamelan elements
- Used on "Crystalline" from Biophilia
- Created specifically for the album's nature/technology theme

**The Gravity Harp:**
- Designed by sculptor Andy Cavatorta
- 25 feet tall with four swinging/rotating pendulums
- 11 strings struck by pendulum motion
- Creates different sequences each performance
- Embodies the album's theme of natural forces as music

**Tesla Coil Synthesizer:**
- Uses electrical arcs to trigger sounds
- Lightning bolts as instrument
- Illuminates stage with blue electricity
- Technology and nature as one

**Production Tools:**
- **Pro Tools**: Used as instrument, not just mixer - "I like that it isn't on a 4/4 grid, and I can be more focused on narration"
- **Sibelius**: For orchestral arrangements (prominent since Vespertine)
- **Melodyne**: Creative pitch manipulation - converted vocal lines to MIDI for orchestration on Vulnicura
- **Yamaha QY20**: Portable sequencer used for sketching in 1990s
- **Reactable**: Visual synthesizer with movable blocks (first musician to adopt for live performance, 2007)
- **Lemur**: Touch screen instrument used on Volta tour

**Domestic Sound Recording (Vespertine):**
> "I recorded all sorts of noises around the house, very quiet ones, and then magnified them up in Pro Tools, and created rhythms with them."

### Methods

**The Biophilia Method:**
1. Identify natural phenomena as musical subjects (crystalline formations, lightning, DNA)
2. Commission custom instruments that embody those phenomena
3. Create app for each song allowing interactive exploration
4. Develop educational workshops for children
5. Perform in unconventional spaces (planetariums, opera houses)

**Multimedia Integration:**
> "For me, it is really exciting to take the cutting edge technology and take it as far as it can get virtually, use it to describe/control the musicology or the behavior of raw natural elements, and then plug it with a sound source which is the most acoustic one there is - like gamelan and pipe organ. So you get the extremes: very virtual and very physical. In that way you shift the physicality."

**VR as New Canvas (Vulnicura):**
- 360-degree videos taking audiences into Icelandic highlands
- Experiments with "inside of Bjork's mouth" VR
- Cited reasons: heightened synaesthesia, 360-degree sound

### Discoveries

- **App Album Format**: Biophilia pioneered the "album as app" concept
- **Custom Instrument Commissioning**: Demonstrated that new instruments can be created for specific artistic visions
- **Educational Music Integration**: Biophilia used in schools to teach music and science together
- **VR Music Experience**: Early adoption of virtual reality for music presentation
- **Reactable Performance**: First musician to integrate visual/touch-based synthesis in live shows

## APPLICATION TO CRE-8

### Principles for Nature-Technology Synthesis

1. **THE UNITY PRINCIPLE**: Nature and technology are not opposites. They are both expressions of creative evolution. Your work should bridge them, not choose between them.

2. **CUSTOM TOOLS FOR CUSTOM VISIONS**: If the instrument you need doesn't exist, build it. Commission it. The gravity harp exists because Bjork needed it to exist.

3. **CREATIVITY AS LIVING THING**: Don't force creativity. Track it with kindness. It moves around - your job is to follow where it goes.

4. **EMOTIONAL ARCHAEOLOGY**: Each project should dig deep into specific emotional terrain. Don't skim across feelings - excavate them.

5. **COLLABORATION AS EXPANSION**: Bring in the world's best in their fields. True collaboration means their discipline transforms yours.

6. **MULTIMEDIA IS ONE MEDIUM**: Sound, vision, touch, interaction - these are not separate arts. They are one art experienced through different senses.

7. **SOUL IN THE MACHINE**: Electronic music has no soul only if you don't put soul into it. Technology is as soulful as the intentions behind it.

### CRE-8 Implementation

- **Custom Tool Development**: When existing tools don't serve the vision, create new ones
- **Cross-Disciplinary Teams**: Bring together experts from apparently unrelated fields
- **Emotional Focus**: Define the specific emotional territory of each project
- **Nature Integration**: Ground digital work in natural processes and metaphors
- **App/Interactive Components**: Consider how work can be experienced interactively
- **Educational Dimensions**: Art that teaches, especially to children

## LEGACY

Bjork proved that pop music can be art, that technology can serve nature, and that multimedia expression need not fragment but can unify. The Biophilia app in MoMA's collection marks a turning point - the first time a music app was recognized as museum-worthy art.

Her custom instruments - gravity harps and tesla coil synthesizers - demonstrate that when vision exceeds available tools, you build new ones. Her VR experiments showed that emerging technology should serve emotional truth, not novelty.

Above all, Bjork proved that the apparent opposition between natural and digital is false. They are the same creative force, experienced at different speeds.

**For CRE-8, Bjork is the patron saint of synthesis.**
