## GLOBAL UNDERSTANDING

**Full Name:** Leonardo di ser Piero da Vinci
**Born:** April 15, 1452, Anchiano, Republic of Florence
**Died:** May 2, 1519, Amboise, Kingdom of France
**Profession:** Polymath - painter, sculptor, architect, musician, mathematician, engineer, inventor, anatomist, geologist, botanist, writer

**NORTHSTAR:** The unified pursuit of knowledge through observation
**Core Belief:** The workings of the human body are an analogy, in microcosm, for the workings of the universe

---

## CHARACTER & MINDSET

### How He Thought
- **Observation First:** Everything began with direct observation of nature
- **Cross-Disciplinary:** Art informed science, science informed art - no separation
- **Documentation:** Compulsive note-taker and sketcher - believed drawing was thinking
- **Reversed Writing:** Wrote in mirror script (right to left) - possibly for secrecy or because he was left-handed
- **Unfinished Works:** Many projects abandoned - curiosity pulled him to new questions

### His Process
1. OBSERVE - Study the subject intensely from nature
2. SKETCH - Draw from multiple angles, in motion, deconstructed
3. ANALYZE - Understand underlying mechanics/anatomy/physics
4. SYNTHESIZE - Apply learnings across domains
5. DOCUMENT - Record everything for future reference

### Key Areas of Study

1. **Anatomy** - Dissected 30+ human bodies, first accurate anatomical drawings
2. **Mechanics** - Gears, pulleys, levers, flying machines, tanks, bridges
3. **Hydrodynamics** - Water flow, waves, canals, diving equipment
4. **Optics** - Light, shadow, perception, camera obscura
5. **Botany** - Plant structures, growth patterns, phyllotaxis
6. **Geology** - Fossils, rock formations, earth history

## FINE-GRANULAR UNDERSTANDING

### The Vitruvian Man (c. 1490)

**What It Is:** Drawing of nude male in two superimposed positions within circle and square
**Source:** Based on architect Vitruvius's description of ideal human proportions
**Location:** Gallerie dell'Accademia, Venice (rarely displayed - light sensitive)

**2025 Discovery:** Researcher Rory Mac Sweeney revealed the equilateral triangle construction method:
- Leonardo's explicit textual reference to "an equilateral triangle" between the legs
- This relates to optimal mandibular function geometry (Bonwill's triangle, 1864)
- Shows Leonardo's precise mathematical approach to human proportions

**Key Proportions:**
- Arm span = Height
- Hand = 1/10 of height
- Head = 1/8 of height
- Face = 1/10 of height

### Sfumato Technique
**Definition:** "Smoke" - Gradual blending of tones without harsh lines
**Application:** Creates atmospheric perspective, soft transitions
**How:** Multiple thin translucent layers of paint (sometimes 30+)
**CRE-8 Application:** Soft UI transitions, gradient blending, depth perception

### Golden Ratio Applications
- Face of Mona Lisa follows phi proportions
- Spiral compositions in many drawings
- Architectural designs use divine proportion
